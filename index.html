About

I’m an engineer in the Digital Health team at Samsung Electronics. I recently completed a Master’s in Data Science and build interpretable models for sleep staging and apnea detection using physiological signals from wearables. My research focuses on explainability (heatmaps, calibrated confidence), cross-signal modeling (SpO₂, DC, HR, accelerometer), and real-world deployment.

I’m currently exploring PhD opportunities in the U.S. and Australia. I enjoy collaborating with clinicians and product teams to turn models into usable tools.

Research

SleepXViT: Waveform Vision Transformers for Sleep Staging with Intuitive Explainability

Preprint / Major Revision — 2024

Transforms multi-channel PSG signals into unified waveform images, enabling vision transformers and consistent heatmap-based explanations. Inter-/intra-epoch transformers improve REM and N1 handling.

paper code demo

Multi-signal Apnea Detection with Cross-Attention & Confidence Calibration

Work in progress — 2025

Combines SpO₂, DC, and HR streams with cross-attention; uses calibrated regression confidence and voting for robust AHI estimation even with unreliable SpO₂.

preprint code

Test-Time Adaptation for Cross-Institution Generalization

Technical report — 2025

Domain-specific batch normalization to adapt models trained on institutions A/B/C to a new site D at inference, improving generalization under distribution shift.

report

Clinician-Centered XAI for Wearable Signals

Methods & case studies — 2024

Heatmaps and calibrated confidence expose low-confidence epochs for quick clinician review, aiding practical deployment of auto-scoring models.
